Project Overview

This project presents an in-depth exploration of global conflicts utilizing the Uppsala Conflict Data Program (UCDP) dataset as its foundation. Through the utilization of SQL as a primary analytical tool, the project aims to uncover and understand the dynamics of conflict prevalence and trends across various regions and years. By delving into the dataset, the project seeks to provide valuable insights into the distribution of violence types and the fluctuation of casualties over time.

The focal point of the analysis lies in the identification of recurring patterns that demand diplomatic attention. By scrutinizing the interplay between conflict incidents, casualties, and geographical regions, the project endeavors to unveil key insights that can guide decision-making processes and the formulation of strategies for enhancing global peace and stability. By deciphering the intricate layers of conflict data, this project contributes to the broader discourse on conflict resolution, equipping stakeholders with the knowledge to navigate the complexities of global conflicts and work towards a more harmonious world.
